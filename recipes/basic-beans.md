---
title: Basic beans
summary: I cook beans like this before I know what I'm doing with them. Eat them on their own or use them in other recipes that call for cooked (or canned) beans. Save the cooking liquid and use it to thicken soups, or in place of vegetable stock.

ingredients:
  - 1 pound dry beans, any kind
  - 1 tablespoon kosher salt (or more or less, to taste)
  - 1 small onion (yellow, white or red will work)
  - 1 bay leaf
  - olive oil or other cooking fat

optional_ingredients:
  - 1 carrot, diced
  - 1 celery rib, chopped
  - garlic, as much as you like
  - herbs, any, as much as you want, fresh or dried
---

**Should I soak beans?** For large runner beans, like Rancho Gordo's Royal Coronas or Scarlet Runners, an overnight soak will speed up cooking time. For smaller common beans, such a yellow eye or black beans, it won't make much difference.

**Always**: Rinse beans and check for rocks or debris.

**To soak**: Place rinsed beans in a container large enough to hold the beans and cover with cold water by at least two inches. Add a tablespoon of kosher salt. Soaking beans can sit on the counter overnight, and they can cook in the same water.

**To cook**: Place a large stockpot or Dutch oven over medium heat and add olive oil or other fat, if using. Add the halved onion, cut side down, so it simmers in the oil for a minute, plus any additional aromatics. (Or skip this step.) If beans have been soaked, add them with their soaking water. Otherwise, add beans and a tablespoon of salt, plus enough water to cover. There should be at least two inches of water covering the beans. Add the bay leaf and any other herbs.

Bring to a boil for 10 to 15 minutes, stir and reduce heat so the pot gently simmers. Partially cover the pot to control evaporation. Cook at a bare simmer for two to three hours, or until the beans are soft but not falling apart. Add water as necessary to keep the beans covered.

Cooked beans can be stored in their cooking liquid for up to a week in the refrigerator, or for six months in the freezer.
