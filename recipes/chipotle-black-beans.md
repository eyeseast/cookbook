  title: Best Black Bean Soup

ingredients:
  - 1 pound dry black beans
  - 1 cup diced carrots
  - 1 cup chopped celery
  - 1 medium onion, chopped
  - 2 - 4 cloves garlic, minced (or to taste)
  - Up to 7 oz (1 small can) chipotle chiles in adobo
  - 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  - 1 or 2 jalapeño peppers, seeded and finely chopped
  - 1 or 2 bay leaves
  - salt
  - black pepper
  - red wine vinegar, to taste
  - cilantro, for garnish
---

Rinse the beans and check them for rocks or other debris. Set aside.

In a large pot or Dutch oven, add olive oil, carrots, celery, and onions, and season generously with salt and pepper. Turn heat to medium and cook, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are softened but not browned, about 10 minutes. Add garlic and jalapeños and cook for another few minutes.

Puree the chipotle chiles with the adobo sauce, either in a blender or on a cutting board. Add to the pot and incorporate, cooking for a few minutes.

Add the beans and enough water to cover by at least two inches. Bring to a boil for 10 to 15 minutes, stir and reduce heat so the pot gently simmers. Partially cover the pot to control evaporation. Cook at a bare simmer for one to two hours, or until the beans are soft but not falling apart. Add water as necessary to keep the beans covered but not soupy.

As the beans finish, let the liquid reduce so that you have saucy beans, not black bean soup. (If you want soup, add liquid.) Serve garnished with cilantro and a splash of vinegar.
